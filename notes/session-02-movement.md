# Session 2 — Movement Without the Ball

**Theme:** Moving to create space, not just to get the ball
**Players:** 7–10 girls, Yr 4–6
**Duration:** 60 mins
**Equipment:** Full court, cones, balls

---

## The Question to Hold All Session

*"Are you moving to get the ball, or moving to create space?"*

---

## Warmup — 10 mins

**Full court layup lines (both sides)**
- Right side first, then left
- Progress from Session 1: add a cone at 45 degrees on each side
- Players must approach from the cone — angle approach, step-gather, finish
- Coaching cue: *"Find the cone first, then attack the basket."*
- Keep lines moving — quick rotation, heart rate up

---

## Block 1 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** The pass-and-move — you're most open right after you pass
- Most beginners pass and stand still — the defender thanks them
- Show it: pass, freeze, watch how easy you are to guard
- Then show: pass, move immediately — defender has to make a choice
- Ask: *"Why are you open right after you pass?"* (Defender watches the ball)

**Drill: 4-corner passing drill with movement**
- They know this drill — now add the rule: after every pass, take 3 steps in any direction before resetting
- Starts to break the habit of passing and planting
- Progress: the receiver can pass back to the mover if they're open
- Coaching cue: *"Pass — then move. Don't admire it."*

---

## Block 2 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** The 3-person weave as a movement lesson
- Revisit the weave from Session 1 — this time focus on what happens after the pass
- The weave only works because every player moves immediately after passing
- Ask: *"What does the passer do after they pass in the weave?"* (Cut behind, fill the outside lane)

**Drill: 3-person weave — movement emphasis**
- Run end to end, finish with a layup — both sides alternating
- Focus: the cut behind after the pass, not the pass itself
- Increase pace each run — by the end it should be near game speed
- Coaching cue: *"Pass and go. No hesitation."*
- Spacing lens: *"Three players, three lanes — nobody stops moving."*

---

## Block 3 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** V-cut — creating space by going toward the defender first
- Brief demo: if you run straight to where you want the ball, the defender goes with you
- The V: step toward defender (sell it), then cut hard to open space
- Ask: *"Why do we go toward the defender first?"*

**Drill: V-cut and receive**
- Two lines: passers on the wing, cutters at the elbow
- Cutter steps toward passer (2 steps), then cuts hard to the corner
- Passer hits them on the move — pass leads them into space
- After receiving, they dribble to the cone and practise the 45-degree layup approach
- Coaching cue: *"Sell the first step. Make the defender believe you."*

---

## Block 4 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Layup angles — introducing the left side
- Review right-side approach briefly
- Introduce left-side layup: same 45-degree angle, opposite hand, opposite foot
- Don't expect clean technique — plant the footwork idea today

**Drill: 2v0 full court — alternating sides**
- Same as Session 1 but alternate: one pair goes right side, next pair goes left
- Ball-handler calls which side before they go
- Partner fills the opposite lane, rebounds, outlets back
- Keep it moving — minimal standing around
- Coaching cue: *"Left side — left hand, right foot up. Walk it if you need to."*

---

## Live Game — 10 mins

**3v3 or 4v4 half court — movement rule**
- One rule added: after you pass, you must take at least 2 steps before you can stand still
- Let them play — call out movement you see, not mistakes
- *"Did you see that cut? That's what we worked on."*

**End question:**
*"When you moved after passing — did it create anything?"*

---

## Coach's Notes

- Who moved naturally after passing without being reminded?
- Who is still planting after the pass — note for individual feedback
- Left-side layup: who needs the footwork broken down slower next session?
- One moment of real off-ball movement to describe in your post-session notes
