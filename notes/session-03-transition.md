# Session 3 — Transition

**Theme:** Running the lanes — attacking before the defence sets
**Players:** 7–10 girls, Yr 4–6
**Duration:** 60 mins
**Equipment:** Full court, cones (mark 3 lanes), balls

---

## The Question to Hold All Session

*"How quickly can we go from defending to attacking?"*

---

## Warmup — 10 mins

**Full court layup lines — both sides, increased pace**
- Both sides, 45-degree approach — this is now the standard warmup
- Progress from Session 2: add a second player trailing for the rebound
- Rebounder outlets immediately to the next group — no waiting
- Coaching cue: *"Rebound — look up — outlet. Don't hold it."*
- This warmup now contains a transition seed: finish, rebound, outlet, go

---

## Block 1 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** The 3 lanes — spacing applied to transition
- Connect directly to what they know from the weave: 3 players, 3 lanes
- Transition is just the weave at game speed with a defence coming back
- Show what happens when everyone runs up the middle (defence collapses easily)
- Ask: *"You've been running 3 lanes in the weave for two sessions. What's different now?"* (Defence. Stakes. Speed.)

**Drill: 3-person weave — full speed, finish strong**
- Run the weave at maximum pace — this is the fitness block
- Finish with a layup on the correct side, alternate each run
- Focus: lane discipline at speed — who drifts to the middle when they get tired?
- Coaching cue: *"Tired is when spacing breaks. Hold your lane."*

---

## Block 2 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** The outlet pass — transition starts with the rebound
- Slow down from Block 1 — teach the trigger moment
- Show: slow outlet = defence sets = no advantage
- Show: quick outlet to the wing = defence scrambling = space exists
- Ask: *"When does the fast break actually start?"* (The moment the ball is secured)

**Drill: Rebound and outlet — full court**
- Coach shoots (miss), 2 players box out
- Rebounder outlets immediately to a guard sprinting to the wing
- Guard pushes into a 2v0 full court layup — both sides
- Rotate through quickly — keep heart rates up
- Coaching cue: *"Catch it and look. The pass is already there."*

---

## Block 3 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** 3v2 fast break — numbers advantage
- This is what good transition creates: more attackers than defenders
- Simple rule: ball goes middle, two outside players fill the corners
- Middle player reads: rim available? Take it. Defender commits? Kick to the corner.
- Ask: *"In a 3v2, who should the defenders stop first?"* (The middle — take away the easiest option)

**Drill: 3v2 fast break drill**
- They know this drill — run it
- First 3 mins: attackers focus on lane discipline and the middle-player decision
- Last 4 mins: defenders add pressure — still a 3v2 but competitive
- Rotate so everyone gets reps attacking and defending
- Coaching cue (attackers): *"Middle — what are they giving you?"*
- Coaching cue (defenders): *"Stop the middle. Make them go wide."*

---

## Block 4 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Layup under pressure — finishing when tired and contested
- By Session 3 they have the basic footwork — now add a trailing defender
- The angle approach matters more when someone is behind you
- Ask: *"Why is the 45-degree angle harder to block?"*

**Drill: 2v1 full court layup**
- 2 attackers vs 1 recovering defender (starts behind)
- Attacker must use correct approach angle — no straight-line drives
- Defender applies token pressure — enough to make them think, not enough to stop them
- Coaching cue: *"Take your angle. Don't let the defender change where you go."*

---

## Live Game — 10 mins

**4v4 full court — transition focus**
- Normal game
- After every made basket or turnover, teams must push immediately — no walking
- Coach acknowledges fast lane-filling out loud: *"Three lanes — did you see that?"*
- One pause allowed: if both teams walk back after a basket, stop and ask *"What did we just miss?"*

**End question:**
*"How many times did you fill a lane before the defence got back?"*

---

## Coach's Notes

- Who is a natural in the middle of the 3v2 — reads the defence, makes the right call?
- Who runs the lane at full pace even when tired?
- Layup under pressure: who slows down too much — needs more confident footwork
- Note one transition moment from the live game to write about this week
