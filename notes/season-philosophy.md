# Season Philosophy — 2026

## Who I'm Coaching

Year 4–6 girls. Beginners in terms of basketball fundamentals, though several have been in the program for a few years. Some individual skills exist but are built on shaky foundations — the priority is correcting those before they become ingrained habits that limit development long term.

These are kids at the start of their basketball education. What I teach them this season, and *how* I teach it, will shape how they think about the game for years.

---

## The One Thing

If these girls finish the season understanding one thing it's this:

**Where to go.**

Not just where to stand — but why. How to read the court, create space for themselves, and make decisions based on what they see rather than what they've been told.

Specifically:
- **Spacing** — understanding where to be and why certain spots on the floor matter
- **Transition** — running the lanes correctly, filling space in attack before the defence sets
- **Team defence** — defending as a unit, not as five individuals; pushing opponents to bad areas, forcing bad passes
- **Defensive adjustment** — the first rotation, when and how to double team, what to do when the first defender is beaten

---

## How I Coach

**Calm and questioning.**

I don't give answers. I ask questions that lead players to find the answers themselves. When something goes wrong on court, the first response isn't correction — it's curiosity. *What did you see? Where did you think the space was? What would you do differently?*

This isn't just a teaching style. It's grounded in something deeper:

**I want these kids to understand their nervous system.**

Sport — especially for young girls — can become a source of anxiety, pressure, and dysregulation. A player who makes a mistake and spirals is harder to coach and harder to reach. I want them to learn, through basketball, how to notice when they're activated and how to come back to ease. Breath, body awareness, not catastrophising a turnover.

The game should feel like play. That's why it's called that.

---

## The Central Question This Season

> Can you teach kids to *see* the game the way good players see it?

Not to run plays. Not to memorise positions. But to develop genuine court vision — the ability to read space, read the defence, and make good decisions under pressure.

That's the experiment. This journal documents the answer.

---

## What I'm Not Trying to Do

- Win at all costs (winning is a by-product, not the goal)
- Overload players with information (one concept at a time, embodied before moving on)
- Coach through fear or correction alone
- Produce technically perfect players who don't understand *why* they're doing what they're doing

---

## The Coaching Mantra

*See it. Feel it. Play it.*

See the space. Feel what your body is doing. Play with freedom.
