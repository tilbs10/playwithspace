# Session 1 — Introduction to Spacing

**Theme:** Spacing as the lens we see every drill through
**Players:** 7–10 girls, Yr 4–6
**Duration:** 60 mins
**Equipment:** Full court, cones, balls

---

## The Question to Hold All Session

*"Where should you be when you don't have the ball?"*

---

## Warmup — 10 mins

**Full court layup lines (both sides)**
- Standard layup lines, right side first
- Focus: footwork only — step-gather, correct foot, soft finish
- Not about making it — about the approach and the steps
- Coaching cue: *"Two steps from the cone. Which foot goes first?"*
- After 4 mins, switch to left side — same focus
- Keep lines moving, keep heart rate up

---

## Block 1 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Why spacing makes every drill easier
- Hold a ball. Ask: *"If I want to pass to you, where's the easiest place for you to be?"*
- Show bunched vs spread — make them feel the difference before naming it
- Introduce the 5 spots briefly — not as rules, just as reference points

**Drill: 4-corner passing drill**
- Players already know this one — use it
- Run it normally for 2 mins, then pause
- Ask: *"What happens to the pass when someone drifts out of their corner?"*
- Run it again with that awareness — same drill, new lens
- Coaching cue: *"Hold your corner. Make the pass easy."*

---

## Block 2 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Spacing in motion — the 3-person weave
- They know this drill. Now show them what it's actually teaching
- The weave works because 3 players fill 3 lanes — nobody bunches
- Ask: *"What breaks the weave?" (Two people in the same lane)*

**Drill: 3-person weave**
- Run it end to end, finish with a layup
- First 3 mins: normal pace, focus on lane discipline
- Last 4 mins: increase pace — players call their own lane if they drift
- Coaching cue: *"Stay in your lane until you pass — then cut behind and fill the outside."*
- Link back: *"This is spacing. Three lanes, three players, nobody bunching."*

---

## Block 3 — 10 mins — **Spacing Focus Block**
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** 5-spot spacing — standing still and seeing the floor
- Slow it down deliberately after the high-pace weave
- 5 players on the 5 spots, no defence, no dribbling
- Ball moves by passing only — every player resets to their spot after each pass
- This is the one block where spacing is explicit, not embedded

**Drill: 5-spot reset**
- 4-pass minimum before any shot
- If spacing collapses, passer holds the ball and waits — no verbal correction needed
- The waiting teaches more than a correction would
- Coaching cue: *"The ball is waiting for you to find your spot."*
- Progress: add one passive defender in the middle if they're ready

---

## Block 4 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Layup angles — where you finish from matters
- Introduce the idea of the 45-degree approach
- Today: just the right side, dominant hand
- Show the cone placement at 45 degrees — this is where the layup starts
- Ask: *"Why don't we go straight at the basket?"* (Angle = easier finish, less contact)

**Drill: 2v0 full court layup — right side**
- Two players, full court, one dribbles the right lane, one fills the left
- Ball-handler finishes right-side layup at 45 degrees
- Partner follows for the rebound, outlets back
- Focus: approach angle and the step-gather — not speed yet
- Coaching cue: *"Find the cone. Two steps. Soft hands."*

---

## Live Game — 10 mins

**3v3 or 4v4 half court**
- Normal game with one coaching rule: if you score from inside the key using correct layup footwork, it counts double
- Let them play — minimal interruption
- Watch for spacing naturally emerging from what they just learned

**End question:**
*"When did you find space today without being told to?"*

---

## Coach's Notes

- Who has natural layup footwork already?
- Who bunches toward the ball in the game — first one to work on
- Did the weave drill create any spacing awareness moments?
- One thing to carry into Session 2
