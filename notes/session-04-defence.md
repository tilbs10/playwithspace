# Session 4 — Team Defence

**Theme:** Defending as a unit — pushing opponents, rotations, first double team
**Players:** 7–10 girls, Yr 4–6
**Duration:** 60 mins
**Equipment:** Full court, cones, balls, bibs (2 colours)

---

## The Question to Hold All Session

*"How do we make the other team go where we want them to go?"*

---

## Warmup — 10 mins

**Full court layup lines — both sides, left-hand emphasis**
- Right side quick (2 mins) — this should be automatic now
- Left side extended (5 mins) — still building the habit
- Add: after the layup, sprint back to half court in defensive stance (lateral slides, not running)
- This plants the defensive transition habit physically before you teach it
- Coaching cue: *"Make the basket, then get back. No walking."*

---

## Block 1 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Channelling — making the ball go where you want
- Show: a defender who mirrors the ball-handler gives them every option
- Show: a defender who overplays one side forces the ball one way
- This is the offensive spacing principle flipped — you're now controlling space, not creating it
- Ask: *"If we want them to go to the sideline, where do we stand?"*

**Drill: 4-corner passing — defensive shadow**
- Use the familiar 4-corner drill but add one defender per corner
- Defender's job: force the receiver to catch on their outside foot only (away from the middle)
- No stealing — just positioning and presence
- Coaching cue: *"Show them the outside. Take away the middle."*

---

## Block 2 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Help defence — when your teammate gets beaten
- Individual defence has limits. When someone gets beaten, the team covers
- Introduce the simple rule: *"Helpside defender steps up, third defender rotates"*
- Keep it to one rotation — don't overload
- Ask: *"If I help my teammate, what happens to my player?"* (Someone has to cover them)

**Drill: 3v3 weave into defence**
- Start with a 3-person weave (they know it) — finish the layup
- After the layup, the 3 attackers immediately become defenders
- 3 new players attack from the other end
- Forces instant defensive transition — they have to communicate and find their player
- Coaching cue: *"Who's got who? Call it out."*

---

## Block 3 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** The double team — trap the corner
- Double teams work when the ball is trapped on the sideline or in the corner
- Two defenders on the ball, other players deny the passing lanes
- Goal: force a 5-second call or a bad pass — not necessarily a steal
- Ask: *"If two of us are on the ball, what do the other defenders do?"*

**Drill: 3v2 fast break into trap**
- Run the 3v2 fast break (from Session 3) — attackers finish or turn it over
- If defence gets the ball, they now have 3 defenders vs 2 attackers coming back
- One of the 3 defenders sprints to trap the ball-handler against the sideline
- Other two deny the pass
- Coaching cue: *"Trap on the sideline. Hands up in the passing lanes."*

---

## Block 4 — 10 mins
*Instruction (3 mins) + Drill (7 mins)*

**Concept:** Full defensive shape — the team moves together
- Connect everything: channelling (Block 1), help (Block 2), trap (Block 3)
- Show the defensive shape: on-ball defender pressures, ball-side denies, weak-side helps
- When the ball moves, the shape moves
- Ask: *"If the ball goes from the top to the wing, what does everyone do?"*

**Drill: Shell drill — 4v4 half court**
- 4 defenders, 4 attackers, no driving allowed yet
- Ball moves by passing only — defenders must adjust on every pass
- Coach pauses once or twice: *"Where should you be right now? Why?"*
- Progress: allow one drive per possession to test the help rotation
- Coaching cue: *"Ball moves — you move. Always."*

---

## Live Game — 10 mins

**4v4 or 5v5 full court — defensive challenge**
- Normal game with one focus: call out any double team that works
- Acknowledge communication: if someone calls *"help"* or *"mine"* — stop and say *"Did you hear that? That's team defence."*
- Finish on a defensive stop if possible — end the session with that feeling

**End question:**
*"What's harder to score against — one defender or five working together?"*

---

## Coach's Notes

- Who communicates on defence without being asked? (Rare and valuable — note it)
- Who ball-watches when the ball isn't near them?
- Did any double team create a turnover or panic? Describe it for your post-session write-up
- Nervous system check: who gets frustrated after being scored on? Good quiet moment for them individually
- What's the one defensive concept to reinforce in Session 5?
